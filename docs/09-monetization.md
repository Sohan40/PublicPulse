# Monetization Plan

Do not monetize before proving that people create, share, and act on real issues.

## Keep citizen reporting free

Free features:
- Create limited public issues.
- Support and verify issues.
- Generate limited complaint drafts.
- Track issue status.
- Share public case pages.

## Best early monetization path

The best early money is not individual citizens. B2C civic apps are hard to monetize.

Start with communities and organizations:

- apartment associations
- resident welfare associations
- college campuses
- local NGOs
- gated communities
- small local admin groups

## Paid community plan

Features:
- Private community board
- Member-only issue reporting
- Admin roles
- Monthly issue reports
- Escalation templates
- Exportable complaint PDFs
- Priority AI draft limits

Possible pricing:
- ₹499/month small group
- ₹1,999/month association
- custom for larger communities

## Premium citizen plan later

Use carefully. Do not paywall basic civic action.

Premium could include:
- more AI complaint drafts
- multilingual drafts
- personal issue tracker
- document exports
- family/locality monitoring

## NGO/journalist dashboard later

Features:
- verified issue discovery
- filters by location/category/severity
- exportable case data
- impact reports

## Authority dashboard later

Hard to sell early but valuable if traction exists:
- authority profile claim
- assigned issue inbox
- public response tools
- resolution analytics

## Data policy

Do not sell personal complaint data. Any insights should be aggregate and anonymized.

## Brutal truth

People may praise this idea but not pay individually. Communities with repeated complaint coordination pain are more likely to pay.
