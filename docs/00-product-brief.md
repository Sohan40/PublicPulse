# Product Brief — PublicPulse

## One-liner

PublicPulse helps citizens turn local civic problems into verified, evidence-backed public cases and AI-assisted complaint drafts.

## Problem

People already complain on WhatsApp, X, Reddit, Instagram, and local groups. The problem is that these complaints are usually unstructured, hard to verify, easy to forget, and rarely tracked to resolution.

Typical flow today:

```txt
Anger -> social post -> comments -> arguments -> forgotten
```

PublicPulse should create a better flow:

```txt
Issue -> evidence -> verification -> complaint -> escalation -> resolution tracking
```

## Initial niche

Start with local civic issues only:

- Road damage
- Drainage overflow
- Garbage collection
- Streetlight failure
- Water supply issues
- Public safety hazards

Do not start with politics, corruption accusations, hospital complaints, workplace complaints, or personal disputes. Those require heavier legal and moderation systems.

## Target users

### Citizens
People who want a proper way to report problems affecting their street, locality, apartment, campus, or city.

### Local community admins
Apartment associations, student groups, locality WhatsApp admins, NGOs, or resident welfare associations.

### Moderators/admins
Trusted users who review high-risk issues and approve public visibility.

### Authorities later
Municipality, panchayat, water board, electricity department, traffic/police, campus admin, or apartment management.

## Core value proposition

- Raise an issue in 60 seconds.
- Attach useful proof.
- AI converts rough text into a clean issue summary.
- Nearby people verify/support.
- AI drafts a proper complaint.
- Status gets tracked publicly.

## What makes it different from X/Reddit

X and Reddit optimize conversation and attention. PublicPulse optimizes evidence, action, and outcome.

| X/Reddit | PublicPulse |
|---|---|
| Posts | Cases |
| Reactions | Support/verification |
| Arguments | Structured timeline |
| Virality | Local relevance |
| Outrage | Resolution progress |
| Follower identity | Citizen impact |

## MVP success metric

The strongest early signal is not signups.

The strongest signal is:

> A user shares a case page outside the app and asks others to support/verify it.

## 30-day beta target

- 50 users
- 20 created issues
- 10 approved public cases
- 5 complaint drafts generated
- 2 issues escalated manually
- 1 real progress/resolution story

## Brutal truth

This product will fail if it becomes a generic feed. It has to be boringly useful: structured complaints, proof, local support, escalation, and tracking.
